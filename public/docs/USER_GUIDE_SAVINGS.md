## ‚ûï Creating a Savings Goal

### Step 1: Navigate to Savings
1. Go to **Finance ‚Üí Savings** in the sidebar
2. View all your current savings goals

### Step 2: Create Goal
1. Click **"Add Savings Goal"** or **"Create Goal"**
2. Fill in the goal information:

#### Required Information:
- **Goal Name**: e.g., "Emergency Fund", "Vacation 2024"
- **Target Amount**: How much you want to save
- **Target Date**: When you want to reach your goal (optional)
- **Current Amount**: How much you've saved so far

#### Optional Information:
- **Description**: Why this goal is important
- **Priority**: High, Medium, Low
- **Category**: 
  - Emergency Fund
  - Vacation
  - Major Purchase
  - Education
  - Retirement
  - Other
- **Notes**: Any additional information

### Step 3: Save
Click **"Save"** to create your goal.

## ‚úèÔ∏è Updating Your Savings

### Adding Money to a Goal
1. Find the savings goal
2. Click **"Add Money"** or **"Deposit"**
3. Enter the amount you're adding
4. Select the source account (if linked to bank accounts)
5. Add optional notes
6. Click **"Save"**

### Withdrawing from a Goal
1. Find the savings goal
2. Click **"Withdraw"**
3. Enter the amount
4. Select destination account
5. Add notes explaining the withdrawal
6. Click **"Save"**

**Note:** Withdrawals reduce your progress toward the goal.

## üéØ Setting Realistic Goals

### Tips for Goal Setting:
1. **Be Specific**: "Vacation Fund $5000" is better than "Save money"
2. **Set Realistic Targets**: Based on your income and expenses
3. **Set Dates**: Deadlines create urgency and motivation
4. **Start Small**: Break large goals into smaller milestones
5. **Review Regularly**: Adjust goals as your situation changes

### Calculating Monthly Savings:
If you have a target amount and target date:
- Total amount needed √∑ months remaining = monthly savings target

**Example:**
- Goal: $12,000 by December (6 months away)
- Monthly target: $12,000 √∑ 6 = $2,000/month

## ‚ùì Common Questions

**Q: Can I have multiple savings goals?**
A: Yes! Create as many goals as you need. Balance priorities carefully.

**Q: What if I can't meet my monthly savings target?**
A: Adjust your target amount, extend the target date, or break the goal into smaller milestones.

**Q: Should I include my emergency fund as a savings goal?**
A: Yes! It's a great way to track your emergency fund growth.

**Q: Can I transfer money between goals?**
A: Withdraw from one goal and add to another - effectively transferring between goals.

**Q: What happens when I reach my goal?**
A: The goal will show as complete. You can mark it as achieved, create a new related goal, or delete it.

**Q: Can I set goals without a target date?**
A: Yes! Dates are optional. Set the amount and work toward it at your own pace.

**Q: How do savings goals relate to my bank accounts?**
A: Savings goals track your savings progress. If you have money in actual savings accounts, link them or update goal amounts when you deposit money.

**Q: What if I need to pause a savings goal?**
A: You can temporarily stop contributing. The goal remains active and you can resume anytime.
