## üìö What is Cash Flow?

Cash flow tracks the actual movement of money:
- **Cash Inflows**: Money coming in (income, loans, transfers)
- **Cash Outflows**: Money going out (expenses, bills, payments)
- **Net Cash Flow**: The difference (inflows minus outflows)

Understanding cash flow helps you:
- **Manage Liquidity**: Ensure you have enough cash
- **Plan Payments**: Know when you can make payments
- **Forecast Future**: Predict future cash position
- **Avoid Shortfalls**: Prevent cash flow problems

## üìä Cash Flow Statement

### Operating Activities
Day-to-day business and personal financial activities:

#### Cash Inflows
- **Income Received**: Salary, freelance income, business revenue
- **Other Income**: Interest, dividends, rental income

#### Cash Outflows
- **Expenses Paid**: Daily expenses, groceries, transportation
- **Bills Paid**: Utilities, subscriptions, recurring bills
- **Other Operating Expenses**: Miscellaneous operating costs

#### Net Cash from Operations
- **Calculation**: Total Inflows - Total Outflows
- **Positive**: More money coming in than going out
- **Negative**: More money going out than coming in

### Investing Activities
Activities related to investments and savings:

#### Cash Inflows
- **Savings Withdrawals**: Money withdrawn from savings
- **Investment Returns**: Returns on investments
- **Asset Sales**: Proceeds from selling assets

#### Cash Outflows
- **Savings Deposits**: Money deposited into savings
- **Investments**: Money invested in assets
- **Asset Purchases**: Purchases of long-term assets

#### Net Cash from Investing
- **Calculation**: Investing Inflows - Investing Outflows
- **Positive**: More withdrawals than deposits (using savings)
- **Negative**: More deposits than withdrawals (building savings)

### Financing Activities
Activities related to borrowing and lending:

#### Cash Inflows
- **Loan Disbursements**: Money received from loans
- **Investment Contributions**: External capital contributions

#### Cash Outflows
- **Loan Payments**: Principal and interest payments on loans
- **Loan Repayments**: Paying back borrowed money

#### Net Cash from Financing
- **Calculation**: Financing Inflows - Financing Outflows
- **Positive**: More borrowing than repayment
- **Negative**: More repayment than borrowing

### Overall Cash Flow

#### Beginning Cash
- Starting cash balance at the beginning of the period

#### Net Cash Flow
- **Calculation**: Operating + Investing + Financing
- Total change in cash during the period

#### Ending Cash
- **Calculation**: Beginning Cash + Net Cash Flow
- Final cash balance at the end of the period

## ‚ùì Frequently Asked Questions

### How often is cash flow calculated?
Cash flow is calculated in real-time as transactions are recorded.

### What's the difference between cash flow and profit?
- **Cash Flow**: Actual movement of money (cash basis)
- **Profit**: Income minus expenses (accrual basis)
- Cash flow can be positive even if profit is negative (and vice versa)

### How do I improve my cash flow?
- Increase income
- Reduce expenses
- Time payments better
- Build cash reserves

### Can I project cash flow for multiple scenarios?
Yes, you can create different scenarios (best case, worst case, likely) to plan for different outcomes.

### What if I have negative cash flow?
Review expenses, increase income, or use savings/reserves. Create a plan to return to positive cash flow.
