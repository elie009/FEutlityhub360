## üìö What is a Balance Sheet?

A balance sheet is a financial statement that shows:
- **Assets**: What you own (cash, bank accounts, investments, etc.)
- **Liabilities**: What you owe (loans, bills, credit cards, etc.)
- **Equity**: Your net worth (assets minus liabilities)

The balance sheet follows the fundamental accounting equation:
```
Assets = Liabilities + Equity
```

## üìä Understanding the Balance Sheet

### Assets Section

#### Current Assets
Liquid assets that can be converted to cash within one year:
- **Cash & Cash Equivalents**: Bank account balances, cash on hand
- **Savings Accounts**: All savings account balances
- **Accounts Receivable**: Money owed to you
- **Other Current Assets**: Short-term investments, prepaid expenses

#### Fixed Assets
Long-term assets:
- **Property**: Real estate owned
- **Equipment**: Business equipment, vehicles
- **Investments**: Long-term investment accounts
- **Other Fixed Assets**: Other long-term assets

#### Other Assets
- **Intangible Assets**: Intellectual property, goodwill
- **Other Non-Current Assets**: Assets not classified elsewhere

### Liabilities Section

#### Current Liabilities
Obligations due within one year:
- **Accounts Payable**: Bills and expenses due soon
- **Credit Card Balances**: Outstanding credit card debt
- **Short-Term Loans**: Loans due within one year
- **Other Current Liabilities**: Other short-term obligations

#### Long-Term Liabilities
Obligations due after one year:
- **Long-Term Loans**: Loans with terms longer than one year
- **Mortgages**: Home loans and property mortgages
- **Other Long-Term Debt**: Other long-term obligations

### Equity Section

#### Owner's Equity
- **Owner's Capital**: Initial capital contributions
- **Retained Earnings**: Accumulated profits/losses over time
- **Total Equity**: Total owner's equity

## üìä Reading Your Balance Sheet

### Key Metrics

#### Total Assets
Sum of all assets:
- Current Assets + Fixed Assets + Other Assets

#### Total Liabilities
Sum of all liabilities:
- Current Liabilities + Long-Term Liabilities

#### Total Equity
Owner's equity:
- Owner's Capital + Retained Earnings

#### Net Worth
Your financial position:
- Total Assets - Total Liabilities = Total Equity

### Understanding the Numbers

#### Positive Equity
- **Good Sign**: Assets exceed liabilities
- **Financial Health**: Indicates positive net worth
- **Growth Potential**: Room for financial growth

#### Negative Equity
- **Warning Sign**: Liabilities exceed assets
- **Action Needed**: Review and reduce liabilities
- **Financial Planning**: Create plan to improve position

## ‚ùì Frequently Asked Questions

### How often is the balance sheet updated?
The balance sheet updates in real-time as you record transactions and update accounts.

### What if my balance sheet is unbalanced?
Check that all accounts are properly recorded and transactions are accurate. Contact support if issues persist.

### Can I view historical balance sheets?
Yes, select any date in the past to view the balance sheet as of that date.

### How do I improve my balance sheet?
- Increase assets (save more, invest)
- Decrease liabilities (pay down debt)
- Maintain positive cash flow

### What's the difference between balance sheet and income statement?
- **Balance Sheet**: Shows financial position at a point in time (assets, liabilities, equity)
- **Income Statement**: Shows financial performance over a period (income, expenses, profit/loss)
